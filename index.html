I am an undergraduate senior studying economics theory and computer science at New York University. I've loved living in Seattle for the past seven years and enjoy the crazy adventure that is living and studying in New York City.


I enjoy my classes, the various projects I'm working on, and bicycling both on the trails at home and on the city streets here in NYC. At NYU, I serve as the president of the Delta Phi chapter of the Phi Kappa Sigma fraternity.


Here's what I've been up to lately:

  • My team won Best Software Project and placed in the top 6 at Hoya Hacks 2016. We built an app called PopMap that helps you plan more comfortable and exciting walking routes. Check out PopMap on devpost.
  • While working on a re-write of my bsh shell in Rust, I began contributing to rustyhorde/readline-sys, libreadline bindings in Rust.
  • Participated in Hacktoberfest 2015 by contributing to killercup/cargo-edit, steveklabnik/glacier, rust-lang/rust, rust-lang/cargo, cmr/this-week-in-rust. All except for cargo-edit were tiny contributions, but I still enjoyed getting to add more ICE tests, fix minor compilation warnings, add a notice to crates.io, and fix a broken link.
  • Co-taught an introductory programming course at my dorm. The code and instructions are on GitHub.
  • My summer internship at Microsoft ended ... but I'm excited to be returning to Microsoft as a full-time software engineer in Fall 2016! I'll be working as a software engineer in the Windows & Devices Group, hopefully on one of the IoT teams.
